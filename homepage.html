DO YOU KNOW THAT ...




Victims keep quiet when they are scammed

A survey by Ipsos Malaysia, involving 1,000 participants, revealed that only 48% of 657 individuals affected by scams reported the incidents to authorities. Surprisingly, 12% took no action. After being scammed, 39% contacted their banks, 36% sought help from friends or family, and 25% shared their experiences on social media. Additionally, 23% filed complaints with consumer protection agencies, and 10% consulted legal advisers. The survey underscores the need for proactive measures, including law enforcement and public education, with increased funding for the National Scam Response Centre (NSRC) seen as a positive step.

30,000+ online fraud cases caused RM1.3 billion in losses (Jan - Nov 2023)

Malaysia faces a surge in online fraud cases, as reported by Bukit Aman, with 32,462 incidents from January to November, resulting in RM1.3 billion in losses. E-commerce fraud, telecommunications crime, and non-existent investment scams are prevalent, marking a 37.5% increase compared to the previous year. Non-existent investment scams alone caused RM421 million in losses. In response to this escalating issue, a collaborative effort named the Scam Free Malaysia Carnival has been initiated by Whoscall Malaysia and Bukit Aman to address the growing threat, aiming to raise awareness and promote vigilance among the public.

VERIFYING INVESTMENT LEGITIMACY - GUIDE FOR MALAYSIA'S INVESTOR


Research the Company

Investigate the company offering the investment opportunity. A reputable company will have transparent information available for scrutiny

Securities Commision

Individuals and companies involved in investment activities must be licensed by the SC. You can verify their licensing status on the SC’s website

Bank Negara Malaysia

For deposit-taking activities, check if the financial institution is regulated by BNM. Visit BNM’s website to ascertain their licensing status


Unrealistics Return

Be cautious if an investment promises exceptionally high returns within a short period or claims zero/low risk.

Reluctance to Share Information

If promoters hesitate to provide detailed company information, financials, or proof for their claims, exercise caution.

Unsolicited Investment Schemes

These are often marketed online or through agents, especially when there’s no verifiable legal presence.
